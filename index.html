Neon ☰

Neon, the best element!

Neon is classified as a non metal and a noble gas. It is a colourless, odourless, and tasteless gas that can change into an orange/red colour in a vacuum tube, it is chemically inactive. It also has the lowest liquid range of any element and is lighter than air. Most of it is trapped deep in the earth.

Molecules that contain neon are the homoleptic Ne.Be2O2.Ne, and heteroleptic Ne.Be2O2.Ar and Ne.Be2O2.Kr.

Neon is commonly used in vacuum tubes to create light and high voltage indicators.

If neon ever directly comes into contact with your skin it can cause frostbite, high exposure can cause fatigue, vision disturbance, headache, and suffocation from lack of oxygen.

Morris Travers and William Ramsay discovered neon in 1893, William Ramsay chilled the neon until it became a liquid and then warmed it up and caught the gas after boiling it off. Nitrogen, Oxygen, and Argon had already been discovered in these samples.

Neon is a very interesting element, I personally find it very cool and fun, but you have to be careful when you are handling it or you can face serious health risks.
